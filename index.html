Who We Are

Over a Century of
Serving Motorists

The Trinidad & Tobago Automobile Association (TTAA) is a non-profit organisation and the premier motoring authority of Trinidad & Tobago. Founded in 1911, we have dedicated over 115 years to protecting and advancing the legitimate interests of our members, while fostering reciprocal relationships with motoring organisations worldwide.

As the official FIA (Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile) member club for mobility and motoring in T&T, we represent this nation in the world's foremost motoring authority — the same body that governs Formula One and global road safety advocacy.

We are a proud partner of FIA Action for Road Safety. The TTAA is led by President Selwyn Persad.

Membership is open from age 17. The TTAA welcomes all T&T drivers — from new licence holders to decades-long motorists.

Our Legacy

A Timeline of Achievements

The TTAA has been first at every critical milestone in T&T's motoring and road safety history. Here is the story of a century-old commitment to excellence.

1911

Association Founded & First Roadside Assistance

The TTAA is established as a non-profit motoring organisation and immediately becomes the first to introduce a roadside assistance wrecking programme to Trinidad & Tobago — setting the template for motorist support in the Caribbean region.

1930

Traffic Lights Introduced to Trinidad

The TTAA becomes the first organisation to introduce and promote the use of traffic lights as part of a structured traffic management system in Trinidad — a foundational leap forward in organising road safety across the island.

1950

Legal Expenses Manslaughter Benefit

Pioneering member protection: the TTAA becomes the first to introduce a Legal Expenses Manslaughter Benefit for its members — providing legal coverage for motorists involved in fatal traffic incidents, a first-of-its-kind initiative in the Caribbean. (Currently not available).

1960

Vehicle Import Standards Partnership

The TTAA becomes the first to partner with the Ministry of Transport – Licensing Division to register and approve all vehicles imported into T&T — establishing critical roadworthiness standards that protect all road users across the country.

1970

Defensive Driving Comes to T&T

A watershed moment: the TTAA becomes the first organisation to introduce, host and offer Defensive Driving classes to Trinidad & Tobago. Over 50 years later, this programme continues to evolve — now featuring world-class motion simulation technology and international FIA recognition.

1977

First & Only IDP Issuer in T&T

The TTAA becomes the first and only organisation authorised to issue International Driver Permits to T&T nationals and CARICOM citizens — enabling legal driving in over 150 countries worldwide. This exclusive authority remains with the TTAA to this day.

1982

Medal of Merit Gold — Road Safety

The TTAA achieves its most prestigious national honour: becoming the first and only automobile association ever awarded the Medal of Merit Gold for Road Safety by the Republic of Trinidad & Tobago. This national award recognises outstanding contributions to road safety and public service.

🥇 Medal of Merit – Gold · Republic of Trinidad & Tobago · 1982
2024

FIA Innovation Challenge — Featured Programme

The TTAA's Motion Simulated Defensive Driving Programme — "Stop the Carnage" — is featured by the FIA in the Innovation Challenge 2024 (Region III) as one of the leading road safety innovations across the Americas and Caribbean, cementing the TTAA's reputation as a world-class driver training institution.

Travel Documents

International
Driver Permit

The International Driver Permit (IDP) is an official document that translates your T&T driver's licence into 12 international languages, allowing you to legally drive in over 150 countries worldwide. The TTAA has been the sole authorised IDP issuer in Trinidad & Tobago since 1977.

An IDP is not a replacement for your national licence — it must be carried alongside it. Together, they give you the legal right to drive in the majority of countries around the globe.
